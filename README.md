# FNSPAY511 Salary Packaging 2022-2023 (Summary)

## Chapter 1: Introduction to Salary Packaging
- **Salary Packaging**: A way for employees to structure their total remuneration, combining cash and non-cash benefits, e.g., superannuation or a company car.
- **Benefits**:
  - Offers flexibility and integrates personal financial planning.
  - Can help employers attract and retain staff.
  - Minimizes employment on-costs like payroll tax and superannuation.
- **Disadvantages**:
  - High administrative costs and complexity.
  - Often limited to high-income employees.

### Eligibility Criteria
- Voluntary and typically requires a minimum employment contract duration.
- Employees should seek professional financial advice before entering salary packaging agreements.

## Chapter 2: Exempt Benefits
- **Exempt Benefits**: Benefits not subject to Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT), such as:
  - Notebook computers.
  - Work-related items (e.g., tools of trade).
  - Childcare provided on employer premises.
  - Minor benefits under $300.
- **Packaging Options**:
  - Employees can package certain items like laptops or superannuation to reduce taxable income.

## Chapter 3: Concessional Organisations
- Public Benevolent Institutions (PBIs), not-for-profit hospitals, and certain other organizations receive tax concessions for salary packaging.
- **Capping Threshold**:
  - PBIs can provide up to $30,000 worth of FBT-free benefits per employee annually.
  - Benefits like car parking are excluded from this cap.

## Chapter 4: Car and Related Fringe Benefits
- **Car Fringe Benefits**: Employers can offer cars to employees as part of their salary packaging. There are two methods to calculate the taxable value:
  - **Statutory Formula Method**.
  - **Operating Cost Method**.
- **Private Use**: Limits apply to private use of company vehicles to qualify for exemptions.

## Chapter 5: Other Benefits
- In-house fringe benefits, housing, entertainment, and residual fringe benefits are some of the common benefits that can be packaged.
- **Living Away from Home Allowance (LAFHA)**: Employers can provide tax concessions for employees living away from their usual residence for work.

## Chapter 6: Relocation and Remote Area Benefits
- Employers may provide tax-exempt relocation assistance and remote area housing benefits.
- **Living Away from Home Allowance**: Exemptions and reductions apply depending on the employee's situation and housing arrangements.

## Chapter 7: Salary Sacrifice of Purchased Leave
- Employees can choose to purchase additional leave as part of their salary packaging, impacting their total employment cost.
